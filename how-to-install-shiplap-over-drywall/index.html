Looking to add rustic charm to your home’s interior walls? Consider shiplap! This on-trend design element is popping up everywhere from living rooms to bathrooms. But how do you install shiplap over drywall?

Pain Points

Installing shiplap over drywall may seem like a daunting task, especially for DIY enthusiasts. You might be wondering about the necessary tools or materials or how to avoid common mistakes.

How to Install Shiplap over Drywall

The process of installing shiplap over drywall is relatively simple but requires precision and patience. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  • First, measure your wall to determine the amount of shiplap needed and purchase accordingly.
  • Remove any outlet covers or other protrusions from the wall.
  • Cut your shiplap to the appropriate size and sand the edges.
  • Attach furring strips vertically to your drywall studs.
  • Starting from the bottom of the wall, attach the shiplap to the furring strips using a nail gun or screws.
  • Continue the process, ensuring each piece is level and evenly spaced.
  • Add trim pieces to cover any visible gaps between the shiplap and adjacent walls or ceiling.
  • Seal any gaps with caulk and finish the shiplap with paint or stain.

Summary

With a few basic tools and some patience, you can transform a room with shiplap walls. Begin by measuring your space and purchasing the appropriate amount of shiplap. Then, attach the shiplap to furring strips on the drywall, ensuring each piece is level and evenly spaced. Finish the look with trim pieces and seal any gaps with caulk.

Adding Personal Experience to How to Install Shiplap over Drywall

When I installed shiplap in my home, I found that sanding the edges of each board helped create a more polished look once assembled. Don’t forget to add extra shiplap for any cuts and waste that might occur!

Tips for Installing Shiplap over Drywall

Here are a few tips to make the installation process a little easier:

  • Measure twice, cut once! This is essential for ensuring your shiplap fits together seamlessly.
  • Use a stud finder to locate the drywall studs and attach furring strips for added stability.
  • Invest in a good-quality nail gun or screws for faster installation.

Shiplap FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about installing shiplap over drywall:

Do you need to remove drywall before installing shiplap?

No, you can install shiplap directly over drywall by attaching furring strips to the studs.

How do you finish shiplap?

Finish shiplap with paint or stain. For a more rustic look, you can lightly sand the surface or leave natural. Be sure to seal any gaps with caulk.

What is the cost of shiplap?

The cost of shiplap varies depending on the type and quality. On average, it costs around $2-$7 per square foot.

Can you install shiplap in a bathroom?

Yes, shiplap is a popular choice for bathroom walls. Be sure to seal any gaps with caulk and consider waterproofing to prevent moisture damage.

Conclusion of How to Install Shiplap over Drywall

Adding shiplap to your walls is a great way to add a touch of rustic charm to any space. With careful planning and execution, you can install shiplap over drywall in no time. Remember to measure carefully, use furring strips for stability, and finish your shiplap with paint or stain. Happy shiplapping!
