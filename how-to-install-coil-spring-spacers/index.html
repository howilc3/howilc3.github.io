If you love driving off-road, or simply want to give your car a more rugged look, then installing coil spring spacers is the way to go. Not only do they give your vehicle a lifted appearance, but they also increase ground clearance, which is useful when navigating rough terrain. In this post, we will walk you through everything you need to know about how to install coil spring spacers, so you can upgrade your vehicle like a pro.

Pain points related to how to install coil spring spacers

Just the thought of installing coil spring spacers can be daunting, especially if you’ve never done it before. It involves jacking up your vehicle, removing the wheels, and compressing the coil springs - all of which require some serious elbow grease. Plus, if not done correctly, it can lead to serious safety issues down the road.

How to install coil spring spacers

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools and equipment. You will need a floor jack, jack stands, a wrench set, a coil spring compressor, and most importantly, a set of coil spring spacers that are compatible with your vehicle make and model. Start by parking your car on a level surface and jacking it up using a floor jack. Secure the car in place with jack stands and remove the wheels. Next, detach the lower control arm and shock absorber from the steering knuckle, then use a coil spring compressor to compress the coil spring. Once the spring is compressed, remove the upper strut mount and slide the coil spring out. You can now slide the coil spring spacer onto the strut, followed by the coil spring. Finally, reassemble everything in the reverse order of disassembly. It is important to note that the process of installing coil spring spacers can differ depending on your specific vehicle, so always refer to your car’s user manual for guidance and follow the manufacturer’s instructions to ensure safety and prevent any damage.

Summary of how to install coil spring spacers

In summary, installing coil spring spacers involves jacking up your vehicle, removing the wheels, compressing the coil spring, and sliding the coil spring spacer onto the strut before reassembling everything. It is important to have all the necessary tools and equipment, as well as follow the manufacturer’s instructions to ensure safety and prevent any damage.

The Pros and Cons of Spring Spacers

Spring spacers are an affordable and easy way to increase ground clearance, which is why they are a popular addition for off-road enthusiasts. One of the biggest advantages is that they allow for larger tire sizes - something that many off-roaders crave to improve performance. However, there are a few cons to consider as well. For example, coil spring spacers can cause your ride to feel stiffer, and may even lead to uneven wear on tires and suspension parts. In addition, they can put additional strain on your car’s suspension, which could lead to costly repairs down the line. Before installing coil spring spacers, it is important to weigh the pros and cons, and to consider factors such as your driving style, vehicle model, and personal preferences.

How to choose the right coil spring spacers

Choosing the right coil spring spacers is crucial to ensuring that your vehicle performs to its full potential. The size and shape of the spacer should match the make and model of your vehicle. In addition, it is important to consider the type of driving you will be doing, as well as the amount of lift you want to achieve. Always opt for high-quality spacers from reputable manufacturers that are made from durable materials and come with a warranty.

How to prevent safety issues when installing coil spring spacers

Safety should always be your top priority when installing coil spring spacers. To prevent safety issues, make sure you have all the necessary tools and equipment, and follow the manufacturer’s instructions to the letter. Never use shortcuts or improvisations, and always use the correct torque specifications. If you’re unsure about any aspect of the installation process, consult a professional automotive technician to ensure that the job is done correctly.

Q: Can I install coil spring spacers myself?

Conclusion of how to install coil spring spacers
