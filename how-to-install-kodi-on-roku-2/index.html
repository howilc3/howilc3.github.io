Pain Points of Installing Kodi on Roku 2

Streaming content on Roku 2 is relatively easy, but it has its limitations. You may find that there are certain apps and channels that aren’t available in the Roku store that you can access via Kodi. This is one of the reasons why some people look for alternative methods of streaming content on their Roku 2 device. How to Install Kodi on Roku 2?

Before we begin, it’s important to note that there is no official way to install Kodi on Roku 2. However, you can use the following steps as a workaround:

  1. First, ensure that both your Roku 2 device and your computer are on the same Wi-Fi network.
  2. Next, go to your Roku 2 device and enable screen mirroring. To do this, go to Settings > System > Screen Mirroring > Screen Mirroring Mode and select “Always Allow”.
  3. Now, download and install Kodi on your Windows or Android device.
  4. Launch the Kodi app and select the “Settings” option.
  5. Select “System” then go to “Display”.
  6. Turn on the “Allow remote control via HTTP” option on the bottom of the screen, and make sure that the “Host name” field is filled in with the IP address of your computer that you noted earlier.
  7. Next, return to the home screen on your Roku 2 device and select “Screen Mirroring”.
  8. Choose your computer from the list of available devices to connect to.
  9. Once you’re connected, your computer’s screen should appear on your Roku 2 device. Now, open the Kodi app on your computer and start streaming content.

Summary

In summary, while it’s not possible to install Kodi directly on Roku 2, you can use screen mirroring to get Kodi to work on your Roku 2. By following the steps above, you can enjoy all the benefits of Kodi on your Roku 2 device, including access to content that might not be available through the Roku store.
