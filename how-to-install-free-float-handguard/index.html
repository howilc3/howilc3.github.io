If you’re looking to improve the accuracy and functionality of your AR-15, one of the most commonly recommended upgrades is installing a free float handguard. Not only does it improve the firearm’s overall stability, but it also allows you to attach accessories like flashlights and lasers. In this article, we’ll be covering everything you need to know about how to install a free float handguard on your AR-15.

The Challenges of Installing a Free Float Handguard

One of the most common challenges you’ll face when installing a free float handguard is disassembling the original handguard. This can be particularly difficult if you’ve never done it before, and even more so if your handguard is held in place with a delta ring. Additionally, installing a free float handguard requires some specialized tools, so it’s important to make sure you have the necessary equipment before you start the project.

How to Install a Free Float Handguard

The first step in installing a free float handguard on your AR-15 is to remove the existing handguard. This typically involves removing the delta ring and the handguard cap, but the specific process will depend on your firearm’s model and the type of handguard you’re installing. Once the old handguard is removed, you can begin installing the new free float handguard. This process usually involves sliding the new handguard onto the barrel nut and attaching screws or bolts to secure it in place.

Summary of How to Install a Free Float Handguard

Installing a free float handguard can be a bit challenging, but it’s a great way to improve the functionality and accuracy of your AR-15. Start by removing the old handguard and then slide the new one onto the barrel nut, securing it in place with screws or bolts as necessary. Be sure to have the necessary tools on hand before starting the project.

Choosing the Right Free Float Handguard for Your AR-15

When it comes to choosing a free float handguard for your AR-15, there are a few key factors you’ll want to consider. First and foremost, you’ll need to decide what kind of attachment system you want to use. Some popular options include keymod, M-LOK, and picatinny rails. Additionally, you’ll want to consider the overall length and weight of the handguard, as well as any additional features like integrated slings and swivels.

The Benefits of Installing a Free Float Handguard

One of the biggest benefits of installing a free float handguard is that it allows for greater accuracy. Because the handguard isn’t attached to the barrel, there’s less torque and movement caused by the various accessories mounted onto it. Additionally, free float handguards typically come with a wider selection of attachment points for accessories, making it easier to customize your firearm to suit your needs.

Choosing the Right Tools for Installing a Free Float Handguard

When installing a free float handguard, it’s important to have the right tools. Some of the most commonly used tools include a torque wrench, a vise block, and a barrel nut wrench. Additionally, it’s a good idea to have some sort of thread locker on hand to ensure that the screws or bolts stay in place once the handguard is installed.

Common Questions About How to Install a Free Float Handguard

Q: Do I Need to Be an Expert to Install a Free Float Handguard?

A: No, you don’t need to be an expert to install a free float handguard on your AR-15. However, you should have some basic knowledge of how your firearm works and be comfortable using tools like a torque wrench and barrel nut wrench.

Q: Is It Necessary to Use Thread Locker?

A: While it’s not strictly necessary to use thread locker when installing a free float handguard, it’s generally a good idea. Thread locker helps to prevent screws and bolts from vibrating loose over time, which can cause the handguard to become unstable.

Q: Will Installing a Free Float Handguard Void My Warranty?

A: It depends on your firearm’s manufacturer and the specific terms of your warranty. Some manufacturers may void your warranty if you make any modifications to your firearm, while others may allow certain types of modifications.

Q: How Much Does It Cost to Install a Free Float Handguard?

A: The cost of installing a free float handguard will depend on the specific handguard you’re using, as well as whether or not you already have the necessary tools. On average, you can expect to pay anywhere from $100-$300 for a quality free float handguard.

Conclusion of How to Install Free Float Handguard

Installing a free float handguard is a great way to improve the accuracy and functionality of your AR-15. Although it can be a bit challenging, it’s a project that can be completed by most gun owners with the right tools and knowledge. By following the steps outlined in this article, you’ll be well on your way to installing a free float handguard and taking your firearm to the next level.
