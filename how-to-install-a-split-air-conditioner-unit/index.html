A split air conditioner unit is an excellent option to cool down a room or a house. Installing it can be a bit daunting, but with the proper guidance, it can be done with ease. In this post, we will be discussing everything about how to install a split air conditioner unit, from start to finish. We will be taking you on a journey through the installation process, pointing out any potential pitfalls, and highlighting the commonly made mistakes. By the end of this post, you will have the foundational knowledge required to install a split air conditioner unit all by yourself.

Pain Points

Many people avoid installing a split air conditioner unit because they think it is a complicated and expensive process. They assume that it involves tearing down walls or making significant changes to their home or apartment. This might be one of the most significant reasons why people shy away from installing this fantastic unit in their homes. But the truth is, the installation process is much simpler than you think, and it won’t cost you a fortune.

Answering the Target

The installation process for a split air conditioner unit is quite straightforward. However, you need to make sure that you have all the required tools and equipment before you begin. Make sure you have a power drill, screws, brackets, a hammer, and a safety ladder so you can access the height required to install your unit. You will also need a vacuum cleaner and a refrigerant kit to finalize the installation process.

Summary of the Main Points

In summary, installing a split air conditioner unit is a straightforward process, and it doesn’t require significant changes to your home or apartment. The installation process can be broken down into four parts: location planning, unit installation, electrical installation, and refrigerant charging. With proper guidelines and equipment, the process can be completed with ease.

Location Planning and Installation

Start by choosing the right location to install your indoor unit. Avoid placing the unit in direct sunlight or any spot where there’s an obstacle that can block the airflow. After choosing the location, drill the holes that match the size of the air conditioner. Use brackets to attach the indoor unit to the wall. Make sure that it is level to the ground. Then, connect the refrigerant pipes to the indoor unit and tighten them with a wrench.

Electrical Installation

The electrical installation is quite simple. First, turn off the circuit breaker that powers the area where you want to install the indoor unit. Then, drill a hole on the wall that connects to the outdoor unit. Pass the electrical wires through the hole and connect them to the indoor unit. Connect the exposed wires to the terminal blocks and cover them with insulation tape. Finally, turn on the circuit breaker and check if the unit is working correctly.

### Refrigerant Charging

The final step in the installation process is refrigerant charging. It must be done correctly, or else the unit won’t function properly. First, turn on the unit and let it run for a few minutes. Then, turn off the unit and connect the refrigerant kit to the outdoor unit. Follow the instructions on the kit to charge the refrigerant. After filling the refrigerant, tighten the valve and make sure there are no leakages.

Maintenance

Once the installation is complete, you need to make sure you maintain the unit to keep it working efficiently. Clean the filters regularly to avoid blockages and accumulation of dirt. Call a professional once a year for a maintenance checkup or if you notice any unusual sounds or smells.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I install a split air conditioner unit by myself?

A: Yes, you can. However, it requires some experience and tools. We recommend calling a professional if you’re not confident in your DIY skills.

Q: How long does it take to install a split air conditioner unit?

A: The installation process usually takes around 4-6 hours depending on your experience level and the complexity of the installation.

Q: How long does a split air conditioner unit last?

A: With proper maintenance, a split air conditioner unit can last up to 15-20 years.

Q: Can a split air conditioner unit cool down a large room?

A: Yes, it can. But it depends on the size of the unit and the room. Consult a professional to choose the right size that would suit your room’s needs.

Conclusion of How to Install a Split Air Conditioner Unit

Installing a split air conditioner unit might seem daunting at first, but with proper guidance, it can be done easily. The installation process involves location planning, unit installation, electrical installation, and refrigerant charging. With the right tools, equipment, and experience, you can install the unit by yourself. However, if you’re not comfortable with DIY, it’s always better to call a professional. With proper maintenance, a split air conditioner unit can last for many years and provide you with a cool and comfortable room throughout the summer season.
