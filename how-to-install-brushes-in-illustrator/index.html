Step-by-Step Guide: How to Install Brushes in Illustrator

Step 1: Download the brushes you want to use.

You can find brushes online easily. Try searching for “Illustrator brushes” to see what’s available. Once you’ve found some brushes you like, download them to your computer.

Step 2: Move the brushes to the correct folder.

Now that you’ve downloaded the brushes, you need to move them to the correct folder so Illustrator can access them. The folder you need to move them to depends on your operating system:

  • Windows: C:\Program Files\Adobe\Adobe Illustrator (version)\Presets\Brushes
  • Mac: Applications/Adobe Illustrator (version)/Presets/Brushes

Make sure you’re moving the brushes to the “Brushes” folder and not the “Brush Libraries” folder.

Step 3: Load the brushes into Illustrator.

Open Illustrator and navigate to the Brushes panel. Click on the menu button in the top right corner and select “Open Brush Library” and then “Other Library”. Navigate to the folder where you saved your brushes and select the file. Your brushes should now be loaded into Illustrator!

FAQ: How to Install Brushes in Illustrator

1. Can I move brushes to a different folder?

Yes, you can. Just make sure you’re moving them to a folder where Illustrator can access them. You can also create subfolders within the Brushes folder to keep your brushes organized.

2. Can I use Photoshop brushes in Illustrator?

No, you can’t. While both programs use brushes, the file formats are different, so they’re not compatible.

3. Do I need to restart Illustrator after installing brushes?

No, you don’t. Once you’ve loaded the brushes into the program, they should be immediately available to use.

4. How do I delete brushes in Illustrator?

To delete a brush, select it in the Brushes panel and then click on the menu button in the top right corner. Select “Delete Brush” from the dropdown menu.
