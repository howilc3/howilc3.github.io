Common Issues with Engines

When it comes to engines, there are a few common issues that can arise over time. For example, as your engine runs, water and oil vapor get mixed up and recirculated back into the engine. This cycle can lead to the creation of a gunk-like substance known as “blow-by.” This blow-by can cause a buildup of unwanted substances in your engine, leading to a decrease in overall performance and even engine damage. Furthermore, if the blow-by is not adequately disposed of, it can mix with your engine’s fuel, causing a host of other issues.

How to Install Oil Catch Can

The solution to these issues is to install an oil catch can. The primary function of an oil catch can is to act as a filter and collect the blow-by. Essentially, the can will trap the oil and water vapor, preventing it from recirculating back into the engine. Of course, like any installation, it is essential to do it correctly. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to install oil catch can:

Step 1: Locate the Right Spot for Your Oil Catch Can

The first step in the installation process is to locate the perfect spot for your oil catch can. You will want to find an area that is easily accessible, as you will need to check and empty the can periodically. Typically, this spot is next to the engine or underneath your hood. Some cars come with pre-designated spots for oil catch cans. If not, look for an area where the oil and water vapor are likely to travel.

Step 2: Remove Excess Tubing

The next step is to remove any excess tubing that may exist. You want to make sure that the oil and water vapor have a clear path to the can without any obstructions.

Step 3: Install the Oil Catch Can

After locating the right spot and removing any excess tubing, it’s time to install the oil catch can. Begin by attaching the inlet and outlet hoses to the can. Ensure that the hoses are attached securely and do not have any room to wiggle or come loose.

Step 4: Connect the Hoses to the Engine

The next step is to connect the hoses to the engine. Locate the PCV valve or any other emission control valve, and connect the inlet hose to it. You may need to remove the valve cover to access the PCV valve. After attaching the inlet hose, connect the outlet hose to the engine’s air intake system.

Step 5: Test Your System

Finally, start your engine and let it idle for a few minutes. While the engine is running, check for any leaks or loose connections. Once you’re satisfied, take your car for a test drive to ensure that everything is working correctly.

How to Maintain Your Oil Catch Can

Like any component in your engine, an oil catch can requires regular maintenance to ensure that it is functioning correctly. Typically, the can will need to be emptied every 1,000 miles, but it’s essential to check it regularly. Here are a few tips on how to maintain your oil catch can:

  • Check it regularly for any oil buildup
  • Empty it regularly to prevent clogs
  • Clean it every few months to keep it functioning properly
  • Replace it once it reaches the end of its lifespan
