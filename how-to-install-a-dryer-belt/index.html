Is your dryer making a loud noise? Does it fail to turn on? These are common issues with a dryer that has a broken or worn-out belt. Replacing the belt might sound like a daunting task, but with the right tools and some patience, you can easily accomplish it. In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of how to install a dryer belt, step by step.

Common Issues with Dryer Belts

When a dryer belt wears out, it can cause several problems. The most common issue is the dryer making a loud thumping noise. This noise occurs when the belt begins to crack or break apart, causing it to slip off track and hit the drum. Another issue is the dryer not turning on at all. A broken belt can cause the drum to stop spinning, which triggers the safety switch and prevents the machine from starting.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Install a Dryer Belt

The first step in replacing a dryer belt is to unplug the machine from the power source. Then, remove the lint trap and the screws that hold the top panel in place. Lift the panel and prop it up with a block of wood. Now, remove the front panel of the dryer by unscrewing the bolts that attach it to the frame.

With the front panel removed, you will have access to the drum and the old belt. Release the tension on the old belt by moving the idler pulley or motor. Once the tension is released, take out the old belt and place the new belt on the drum in the same position as the old one. Make sure the ribbed side of the belt is facing inward towards the drum.

Loop the belt over the motor and the idler pulley and apply tension. The tension should be tight enough, so the belt doesn’t slip but not too tight to cause damage to the motor or pulley. With the new belt in place, reassemble the front panel, top panel, and lint trap.

Summary of How to Install a Dryer Belt

To summarize, replacing a dryer belt involves disconnecting the power supply, removing the front and top panels to access the drum and old belt, releasing the tension on the old belt, replacing the old belt with a new one, looping the new belt over the motor and idler pulley, applying tension, and reassembling the panels and lint trap. Remember to ensure the ribbed side of the belt faces the drum’s inward side.

How to Install a Dryer Belt - Tips and Tricks

Replacing a dryer belt can be a tricky task. Here are some tips to help you get the job done:

  • Before starting, ensure you have the correct replacement belt by checking the model number of the dryer.
  • Take a picture of the belt configuration before removing the old belt so that you refer to it while installing the new one.
  • When releasing tension on the old belt, take a picture of the routing configuration, so you know how to route your new belt over the pulleys.
  • While installing the new belt, ensure that the drum doesn’t move, or you’ll have to start from scratch.

Importance of a Properly Installed Dryer Belt

A dryer belt, when installed correctly, ensures that the drum rotates smoothly, generating enough heat to dry clothes efficiently without making loud noises or causing the machine to fail. A bad belt arrangement can cause further damage to other components like motor or pulleys, leading to expensive repairs.

Common Dryer Belt Installation Mistakes to Avoid

Some common mistakes to avoid when installing a dryer belt are:

  • Routing the new belt differently from the old one.
  • Installing the belt too tight, causing unnecessary pressure on the motor and pulley.
  • Installing the belt too loose, causing the drum to slip and make noise.

Question and Answer Section

Q. Can I replace the dryer belt myself?

A. Yes. Replacing a dryer belt is a DIY task that can be accomplished with a few simple tools and some patience. Follow the instructions in this post and refer to the user manual for your dryer’s specific instructions.

Q. How often should I replace my dryer belt?

A. A dryer belt can last between five to ten years depending on usage. If you notice any signs of wear and tear like cracks or fraying, it’s time to replace the belt.

Q. What’s the typical cost of replacing a dryer belt?

A. The cost of replacing a dryer belt varies based on the brand and model of the dryer. Typically, the price ranges between $10 to $25.

Q. Can a broken dryer belt damage the dryer?

A. Yes. A broken or worn-out belt can cause the drum to stop spinning, causing further damage to the motor or pulleys.

Conclusion of How to Install a Dryer Belt

Replacing a dryer belt is an easy and affordable DIY task that can save you money and prevent further damage to your dryer. Remember to follow the instructions in this post and refer to your user manual for your dryer’s specific requirements. Don’t forget to take safety precautions like disconnecting the power supply before you start and wearing gloves to protect your hands.
