If you’re looking for a reliable and safe source of electricity in your garage, installing a subpanel might be just what you need. With a subpanel, you can power all of your tools and equipment without overloading your existing circuits.

Installing a subpanel in your garage can be a daunting task, especially if you don’t have a lot of electrical experience. From understanding the electrical codes to selecting the right materials, there are many pain points that can make the process overwhelming.

However, with the right knowledge and preparation, you can install a subpanel in your garage safely and efficiently.

In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to install a subpanel in garage and answer some common questions about the process.

First, let’s go over the basics of what a subpanel is and why you might need one.

What is a subpanel and why do I need one in my garage?

A subpanel is an additional electrical panel that is connected to your main electrical service panel. It allows you to add more circuits and can be used to power a large amount of electrical devices.

If you have a detached garage or workshop that is far away from your main circuit breaker panel, adding a subpanel is a good idea. It will provide you with a separate source of electricity that you can use to power all of your tools and equipment without overloading your main panel.

Additionally, if you are planning to add more electrical devices or appliances to your garage in the future, having a subpanel installed will accommodate those needs and prevent overloading your circuits.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Install a Subpanel in Garage

Before you begin the installation process, you’ll need to determine the electrical load that will be placed on the subpanel. This will help you select the appropriate size and amperage for the subpanel and ensure that it can handle the electrical demand of your garage.

Here are the basic steps to follow for installing a subpanel in your garage:

Step 1: Turn Off Power to the Main Breaker Box

Before installing a subpanel, you’ll need to turn off the power to your main breaker box to avoid electrocution. Use a circuit tester to make sure that all circuits are off and there is no live electrical current.

Step 2: Plan the Subpanel Placement

Determine the location where you want to install the subpanel. Make sure it’s easily accessible and there is enough space for the panel and wiring.

Step 3: Run the Wiring from the Main Panel to the Subpanel

Run the appropriate sized cable from your main breaker box to the location where you plan to install the subpanel.

Step 4: Install the Subpanel

Install the subpanel in the desired location and connect the cables to their appropriate circuits.

Step 5: Connect the Circuits to the Subpanel

Connect each circuit to the subpanel’s circuit breakers. Make sure you follow proper wiring and grounding techniques.

Step 6: Turn on the Power

Once everything is wired and connected correctly, turn on the power to your main breaker box. Check all circuits and make sure they are working correctly before using the subpanel.

FAQs about How to Install a Subpanel in Garage

1. How many amps should a subpanel in a garage have?

The number of amps needed for a subpanel depends on the electrical load of the garage. On average, garages usually require a minimum of 60 amps, but larger garages or workshops may require up to 100 amps.

2. Can I install a subpanel in my attached garage?

Yes, you can install a subpanel in your attached garage. However, you’ll need to follow electrical codes and regulations for safety purposes.

3. Can I add a subpanel to my existing garage circuit?

No, you cannot add a subpanel to an existing garage circuit. You’ll need to install a new circuit and subpanel to avoid overloading your existing circuit and possibly causing a fire.

4. Do I need a permit to install a garage subpanel?

Yes, you will need a permit from your local building department to install a garage subpanel. Electrical work can be dangerous, and permits help ensure that the work is done safely and up to code.

Conclusion of How to Install a Subpanel in Garage

Installing a subpanel in your garage can give you the added electrical power you need to accommodate your tools and equipment without compromising your safety. By following the above steps and electrical codes and regulations, you can ensure that the installation is done safely and efficiently.
